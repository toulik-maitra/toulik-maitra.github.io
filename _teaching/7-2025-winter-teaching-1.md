This course was outside both my department and my primary research area. That was part of why I wanted to do it. Biological process control sits at the intersection of engineering, biology, and programming in a way that does not belong cleanly to any one discipline, and teaching at those intersections is where I learn the most.

I ran hands-on MATLAB sessions where students implemented control systems algorithms and simulated biological feedback systems. I also led weekly workshops on applying control theory to biological problems and worked closely with students on final projects that involved building and analyzing their own models.

Topics included mathematical modeling of biological processes, feedback control systems, stability analysis, and PID controller design. Teaching MATLAB to students learning it for the first time while using it for real engineering tasks meant staying close to where people were actually getting stuck, not just where the curriculum assumed they would be.
