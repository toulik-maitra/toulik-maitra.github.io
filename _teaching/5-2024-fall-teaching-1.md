Four online discussion sections per week. The hardest thing about online teaching is that the natural feedback of a physical room disappears. You cannot read a room through a screen. I got better at asking questions that surfaced confusion before it hardened, and at noticing when a session had gone quiet in the wrong way.

The course covered general chemistry fundamentals from unit conversion and stoichiometry through chemical equilibria. Most students were in their first semester of university. For a lot of them, this was their first experience of a science course moving fast. Part of the job was helping them keep pace without making it feel like they were falling behind.
