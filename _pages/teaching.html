I have taught 9 courses across 4 departments at UC Davis, from freshman general chemistry to upper-division fluid mechanics and materials science. Teaching outside my own department was a deliberate choice. It sharpened how I think about explanation.

The goal is always the same. Make abstract equations feel concrete. Students retain things better when they watch a concept operate in something physical before meeting it in symbols. Across these courses I've worked with hundreds of students, in every format from lab sections to online review sessions. Course evaluation PDFs are linked in each entry below.
