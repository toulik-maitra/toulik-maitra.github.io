I have taught 7 courses across 4 departments at UC Davis, from freshman general chemistry to upper-division fluid mechanics, control systems, and materials science. Teaching outside my own department kept happening because I kept saying yes to it. That turned out to be worth it.

The consistent goal across all of it has been making abstract equations feel concrete. Students retain things better when they have watched a concept operate in something physical before they encounter it in symbols. Across these courses I have worked with hundreds of students in lab, discussion, review, and project sessions, online and in person. Course evaluation PDFs are linked in each entry below.
