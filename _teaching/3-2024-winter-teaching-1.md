General chemistry has a reputation as the first filter in a science degree. I did not teach it that way. Most of these students were in their first or second year, working out whether they belonged in a science program at all. Getting the material across clearly was not separate from that question.

I held two discussion sessions per week covering thermochemistry, chemical bonding, and molecular orbital theory, and supervised weekly lab sessions on experiments including freezing point depression and boiling point elevation. Teaching this course while preparing for my own qualifying exams the same semester helped. I was thinking about the same underlying chemistry from two very different angles at once.
