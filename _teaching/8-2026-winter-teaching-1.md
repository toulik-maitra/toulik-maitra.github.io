Chemical reactor design is where the ChemE sequence converges. The physics is familiar by then. What changes is the question: given what you know about kinetics and transport, how do you build something that makes product?

I ran weekly discussion sections covering ideal reactor models, residence time distributions, non-ideal flow, and multiple reaction systems. Most of the office hours work involved problems that required choosing between reactor geometries rather than solving for a prescribed one.

Choosing a reactor is what the harder problems require. Students who understood that each model encodes a physical assumption about mixing did well on those. The ones who had memorized the design equations without asking what they assumed struggled when that assumption had to be made.
